How Much Does It Cost to Rekey Locks in Texas? (2026)

By Right Choice Locksmith · Updated April 2026

If you've just moved into a new home, lost a key, or had a roommate situation change, rekeying your locks is one of the smartest (and most affordable) security upgrades you can make. But how much does it actually cost in Texas?

Here's a straightforward breakdown based on what we charge and what's typical in the Austin and Dallas markets.

Average Rekeying Costs in Texas (2026)

Service call / trip charge: $35–$75

Per lock rekeying (standard): $15–$30

Whole house (3–5 locks): $100–$200

Commercial rekeying (per lock): $20–$50

Most Austin and Dallas homeowners pay between $100 and $175 to have a full house rekeyed (3–5 doors), including the service call.

What Factors Affect the Price?

  • Number of locks — More locks means more time and more pins. Most locksmiths charge per lock after the service call.
  • Lock brand and type — Standard Kwikset and Schlage locks are the easiest and cheapest to rekey. High-security locks (Medeco, Mul-T-Lock) take longer and cost more.
  • Time of day — Some locksmiths charge a premium for after-hours calls. We don't.
  • Your location — Urban Austin and Dallas are typically priced similarly. Rural areas may have a higher trip charge.
  • Number of keys needed — Additional key copies are typically $3–$8 each.

Rekeying vs. Replacing Locks — Which Should You Choose?

Rekey if: Your lock hardware is in good condition and you just want to change who has access. Rekeying is faster, cheaper, and just as secure as a full replacement.

Replace if: Your lock is damaged, worn out, or you want to upgrade to a higher security grade or smart lock. Replacement makes sense when the hardware itself is the problem.

For most Texas homeowners who just moved in or lost a key, rekeying is the right call — you'll spend 50–70% less than you would on new locks.
