Best Smart Locks for Texas Homes in 2026 — Locksmith Approved

By Right Choice Locksmith · Updated April 2026

Texas weather is hard on everything — including your door locks. Summer temperatures above 100°F, high humidity in Houston and Austin, and occasional winter freezes in Dallas mean your smart lock needs to be built for extremes. Here's what we actually recommend after installing hundreds of smart locks across Texas.

What to Look for in a Texas Smart Lock

  • Operating temperature range — Look for locks rated to at least 32°F–130°F. Texas summers regularly hit 105°F+ and door surfaces get even hotter.
  • IP or ANSI rating — At minimum, ANSI Grade 2. For exterior doors, Grade 1 is ideal.
  • Battery life — With Texas heat draining batteries faster, look for locks rated 6–12 months. Make sure they have a low-battery warning.
  • Keypad backup — Always have a physical keypad in case the app or Wi-Fi fails.

Our Top Picks

1. Schlage Encode Plus — Best Overall

The most-installed smart lock in our fleet. Built-in Wi-Fi (no hub required), Apple Home Key support, ANSI Grade 1 rating, and handles Texas heat without issue. The keypad is backlit and responsive even in summer glare. Works on standard door preps.

2. Kwikset Halo Touch — Best for Fingerprint Entry

If you want fingerprint unlock, the Halo Touch is our pick. It's fast, reliable, and rated for Texas temperatures. Works with Google Home and Amazon Alexa. We've seen good longevity on these in Austin installs. The SmartKey rekey feature is also handy.

3. Yale Assure Lock 2 — Best for Renters / Retrofits

Works with your existing deadbolt — no exterior hardware change required. Perfect if you're renting, in an HOA, or just don't want to drill new holes. Connects via Z-Wave or Zigbee (hub required) or the Yale Access app directly. Slim and clean-looking.

4. Schlage BE489WB — Best Budget Pick

If you want the reliability of Schlage without the premium price, the BE489WB hits the mark. Z-Wave smart home connectivity, ANSI Grade 2, and a proven track record. We've installed dozens in Dallas and have had very few callbacks.

What We Avoid

We generally steer Texas homeowners away from cheap off-brand smart locks. The metal components corrode faster in humid central Texas conditions, the keypads fail in extreme heat, and the app support disappears after 2–3 years when the company moves on.

Stick with Schlage, Kwikset, Yale, or Level — these brands stand behind their products and have parts available.

Professional Installation Matters

A smart lock is only as good as its installation. Misalignment causes the bolt to bind, drains batteries faster, and eventually kills the motor. When we install a smart lock, we check door alignment, adjust the strike plate if needed, and test the lock through dozens of cycles before we leave.
