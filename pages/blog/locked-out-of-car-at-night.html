Locked Out of Your Car at Night? Here's Exactly What to Do

By Right Choice Locksmith · Updated April 2026

It happens to everyone at some point — you're standing in a dark parking lot, keys locked inside your car, phone at low battery, and it's late. Here's exactly what to do, step by step.

Step 1: Stay Calm and Move to a Safe Location

Your first priority is personal safety, not the car. If you're in an isolated or unsafe area, move toward a lit, populated space — a store entrance, a restaurant, anywhere with people and lighting. Don't stand alone in a dark parking lot.

Step 2: Check Your Options

Before calling anyone, do a quick check:

  • Try all doors — sometimes a back door or the trunk is unlocked.
  • Check if you have a spare key at home or with a trusted person nearby.
  • Check if your vehicle has a remote app (many modern vehicles have manufacturer apps that can unlock remotely).

Step 3: Call a Locksmith — Not a Tow Truck

This is the most important advice: call a locksmith, not a tow truck. A tow truck can't unlock your car — they'll take it to a dealership, which means hours of waiting and a bill that could be 3–5x more expensive than a locksmith.

A licensed automotive locksmith will come to your exact location, open the car without damage, and be done in minutes. In Austin and Dallas, expect a 30-minute response from Right Choice Locksmith.

Step 4: What NOT to Do

  • Don't try to use a coat hanger or slim jim yourself — modern cars have airbag sensors in the door frame. You can trigger an airbag and cause serious injury or expensive damage.
  • Don't break a window — replacing a car window typically costs $200–$500. A locksmith costs a fraction of that.
  • Don't accept help from strangers — unfortunately, some people will offer to help and use it as an opportunity for theft or worse.

What to Tell the Locksmith When You Call

Have this information ready to speed things up:

  • Your exact location (cross streets, parking lot name, or drop a pin)
  • Your vehicle year, make, and model
  • Whether the keys are visible inside the car

This lets the locksmith bring the right tools and give you an accurate ETA and price before arriving.

How to Prevent It from Happening Again

  • Keep a spare key at home or with a trusted person.
  • Consider a magnetic key holder hidden under your vehicle.
  • Download your vehicle manufacturer's app if it supports remote unlock.
  • Save a local locksmith's number in your phone — you won't regret it at 2am.
